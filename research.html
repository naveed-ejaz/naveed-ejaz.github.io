Research

My research focuses on applied multimodal artificial intelligence for intelligent decision-making in real-world systems. I develop AI methods that integrate visual, textual, temporal, and structured data to support evidence-based decisions in healthcare, infrastructure, and societal systems. A central aim of my work is to ensure AI solutions are deployable, trustworthy, and impactful, rather than purely theoretical.

Multimodal AI Vision–Language NLP / LLMs Geospatial & CV Responsible AI Real-world deployment

Key Research Areas

AI for Health & Wellbeing

I develop machine learning and multimodal AI systems for clinical decision support, risk assessment, and automation of healthcare workflows. This includes the use of large language models for clinical documentation and information extraction, with attention to privacy, robustness, and safe deployment in real healthcare settings.

AI for Smart Infrastructure & Planning

My work applies computer vision and geospatial AI to the monitoring and analysis of the built environment. Using drone and aerial imagery and temporal data, I develop methods for road condition assessment, infrastructure monitoring, and urban and environmental planning, supporting decision-making in public-sector contexts.

Responsible AI for Safety & Society

I investigate AI approaches for online safety, harm detection, and ethical AI deployment, including violence and cyberbullying detection and explainable AI. This work focuses on aligning AI systems with societal values, legal frameworks, and ethical principles, particularly in safety-critical and public-sector applications.

Multimodal & Generative AI Systems

Across applications, I study multimodal and generative AI, including vision–language models, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and hybrid edge–cloud AI systems. These methods enable AI systems to reason across multiple data sources and underpin my applied research.

Methods & Expertise

  • Machine learning and deep learning
  • Computer vision and image analysis
  • Natural language processing and large language models
  • Multimodal data fusion
  • Explainable and responsible AI
  • Real-world AI deployment and evaluation

Impact & Future Direction

My work has led to AI systems deployed or evaluated in real-world settings, including healthcare services, public-sector organisations, and industry partners. Over the next five years, my research will strengthen the foundations and applications of applied multimodal AI in areas of strategic importance to the UK and Northern Ireland, particularly healthcare transformation, smart infrastructure, and responsible AI for society, through interdisciplinary collaboration and impact-driven research.
